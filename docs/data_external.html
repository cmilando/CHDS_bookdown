External Data Resources

These tables summarize external data platforms that provide secure access to clinical, claims, and population data for large-scale research. These resources often require special credentials or affiliations and offer opportunities for collaboration and advanced analytics.

Resource Description
VA Informatics & Computing Infrastructure (VINCI) Secure VA-managed environment offering access to national EHR data for 9M+ veterans, including structured/unstructured data, pharmacy, imaging, and claims. Used in chronic disease and aging research.
All of Us Research Program NIH initiative collecting EHR, biospecimen, survey, and wearable data from 1M+ participants. Focus on diversity, longitudinal tracking, and personalized medicine via a secure Researcher Workbench.
Acumen CMS Data Linkage (NIA) Links Medicare claims to NIA-funded studies (e.g., HRS, NHATS). Enables longitudinal analysis of older adults’ health, spending, and outcomes. Managed by Acumen LLC with curated, linked datasets.
OptumLabs Data Warehouse Large dataset of claims + EHR for 200M+ patients, used in real-world evidence research. Strong linkage, cost/utilization analysis; access is proprietary and costly.
TriNetX Federated EHR network used for real-time cohort building, feasibility assessment, and clinical trial recruitment. De-identified data from 120+ global healthcare orgs.
ADVANCE Clinical Research Network (OCHIN) PCORnet network aggregating EHR and claims data from safety-net providers, focusing on underserved populations. Uses PCORnet CDM. Strong for health equity and primary care research.
Informatics for Integrating Biology & the Bedside (i2b2) Open-source platform for EHR cohort discovery at academic centers. Provides aggregate patient counts for exploratory work. Customizable, but no direct access to patient-level data.
National Clinical Cohort Collaborative (N3C) NIH-sponsored data enclave with harmonized EHR from 80+ institutions. Initially focused on COVID-19, now expanded. Supports ML and advanced analytics with OMOP-standardized data.
OHDSI (Observational Health Data Sciences and Informatics) Global open-science network using the OMOP data model to enable distributed observational research across EHR and claims data. Tools for comparative effectiveness and large-scale phenotyping.
